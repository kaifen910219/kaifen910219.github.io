About

Concert Pianist | Distinguished Scholar | Dedicated Educator

Born in 2002 in Taiwan, I am Alice Tung‚Äîa concert pianist who has captivated audiences through numerous performances across Taiwan and the United States. Currently pursuing a Master of Music in Piano Performance at CUNY Brooklyn College under the distinguished guidance of Professor Beatrice Long, I have been honored with merit-based scholarships for two consecutive years, reflecting my commitment to artistic excellence and academic achievement.

My musical journey began at an early age through Yamaha music education, where I developed a comprehensive foundation in harmony, improvisation, and aural skills‚Äîrigorous training that continues to inform my artistry today. This solid foundation has enabled me to give countless solo recitals, compete successfully in international competitions, and perform as a concerto soloist with orchestras.

As an experienced educator with 8 years of teaching experience, including nearly 2 years in New York City, I am passionate about nurturing the next generation of musicians. I bring the same dedication to my teaching as I do to my performances, helping students discover their unique musical voices while building strong technical foundations.

Recently awarded as the CUNY Brooklyn College Concerto Competition Winner, I am actively performing and competing at the highest levels. Looking ahead, I am committed to establishing myself as a professional concert pianist and plan to pursue an Artist Diploma or Doctorate in Musical Arts (DMA), the pinnacle of performance credentials, to further refine my artistry and deepen my contribution to the classical music world.
